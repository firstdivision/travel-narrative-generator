## Introduction

This trip is meant to be a travel log for all of Asia, or at least my attempt at making sense of it as I go.

I want to keep a record of the movement, the logistics, the places themselves, and whatever happens in between. Not a polished version, not the neat summary you invent afterward when the rough edges have already been sanded off, but something closer to how travel actually feels while it is happening. A little uneven, occasionally confusing, sometimes tiring, and, if I am paying attention, full of details that would otherwise disappear.

That is the plan, anyway.

Asia is too large, too varied, and too contradictory to pretend I am going to understand it in any complete way. So this is not that kind of project. It is just a running account, one day at a time, of where I went, what I noticed, what went wrong, what surprised me, and what stayed with me by the end of the day.

I expect there will be long transit days, minor mistakes, overheated afternoons, stations and airports that blur together, and moments that feel sharper than they should for no obvious reason. Those are usually the parts worth writing down.

So this is the beginning.
