## Thursday, April 23 - Cardamom, Rain, and Impulse

I went to work, then had lunch with the work crew at a new restaurant called India Portal. I ordered a coffee drink whose name I of course cannot remember, but it was definitely made with cardamom, which is possibly one of the best spices in the world. Anyone who knows me knows my family makes cardamom bread, and when one person was first handed a fresh warm loaf by my mother, his reaction was basically, "this is the best [expletive] bread you've ever had in your life!" I still think that was a fair review.

At the restaurant I also ordered a braised lamb shank, which came with two kinds of rice. For some reason I accepted this arrangement without protest, even though I have been getting a little tired of rice, mostly because so many meals here arrive with rice, or are simply dominated by it. This dish was no exception. The piles of rice dwarfed the single lamb shank on the plate, and try as I might, I could not finish either the white rice or the biryani rice. Still, it tasted excellent, especially the lamb shank, tender and covered in a dark, rich sauce that matched the rice very well.

Later that night I met up with my new friend to go on a Kuala Lumpur night bus tour. It was the same bus that normally runs during the day for the hop-on hop-off city tours, but at night it becomes a roughly two-hour drive past many of the city's highlights, with two stops where you can get off, walk around, and take pictures.

About twenty minutes before the bus was supposed to arrive, a tremendously loud crack peeled across the sky, which could only mean one thing. Thunder and rain were coming. We were not disappointed. Shortly after that first blast of thunder and lightning, the sky opened up in torrential sheets that turned the road into a river. Everybody waiting for the bus cowered under an overhang together, and several of us still braved the downpour long enough to take a quick video for social media. Not even torrential rain can stop us from posting. In fact, that may be when people are at their most committed.

When the bus finally arrived, my friend and I got seats on the inside because it was still raining. Soon enough the rain stopped, so we migrated upstairs to the open top section and found seats that were, yes, still a bit wet. We wiped them down with our hands as best we could and sat anyway.

The bus rolled past many of Kuala Lumpur's landmarks, the KL Tower, the Petronas Towers, KLCC, and a temple whose name I was completely blanking on and hoped my photos would later remind me of. At night the city felt sharper somehow. Glass and steel reflected everything back at itself, and the wet streets held onto the lights a little longer.

After the tour we walked around central KL for a while and found ourselves in the middle of live music. Two different bands, actually, were playing at intersections, with drums and voices drifting across the roads, bouncing off the buildings, and pulling us farther into the crowd to see what was going on. At some point we found a shawarma place and ordered shawarma fries and a soda for takeaway. Yes, takeaway. Not "to go." I am adapting, at least linguistically.

Later that night, or really the next day at around 2 AM, I decided that since I had no plans for the weekend, and it was now Friday, I had better make some. This decision was helped along by texts from family and friends back home who very strongly suggested I should go to Singapore for the weekend. So I did. I booked a flight and a hotel at two in the morning on Friday, then went to sleep for a few hours before getting ready for work.
