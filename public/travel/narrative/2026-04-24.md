## Thursday, April 23 - Rain, Rice, and Impulse

I went to work, then had lunch with the work crew at a new restaurant called India Portal. I ordered some kind of coffee drink whose name I of course cannot remember, but it was definitely made with cardamom, which is possibly one of the best spices in the world. Anybody who knows me knows my family makes cardamom bread, and when people try it warm for the first time the reaction tends to become immediate and profane. One person, after my mother handed him a fresh loaf, declared it the best bread he'd ever had in his life, with emphasis.

So the coffee had my full attention from the start.

I also ordered a braised lamb shank, which arrived with *two* kinds of rice. For some reason I accepted this arrangement without protest, even though I had already started getting a little tired of rice, mostly because so many things here come with rice or are almost entirely rice. This dish was no exception. The plate looked slightly absurd, the piles of white rice and biryani rice dwarfing the single lamb shank like they had entered a different competition. I tried, but I could finish neither. Still, it tasted excellent. The lamb was tender, and the dark, rich sauce matched the rice perfectly. Good enough that I kept eating long after common sense had made its recommendation. But the spiced coffee, cardamom and whatever else was in it, stole my heart.

Later that night I met up with my new friend for a Kuala Lumpur night bus tour. It was the same bus used for the daytime hop-on hop-off city tour, but at night it became a roughly two-hour drive past the city's highlights, with two stops where we could get off, walk around, and take pictures.

About twenty minutes before the bus was supposed to arrive, there was a tremendously loud crack across the sky. That could only mean one thing. Thunder and rain were on the way. They did not disappoint. Soon after that first burst of thunder and lightning, the rain came down in torrential sheets, hard enough to turn the road into a river. Everyone waiting for the bus packed themselves under an overhang. One by one, each of us still ventured briefly into the downpour to take a quick video, because apparently even a storm like that is not enough to interrupt the urge to post something. Maybe especially then.

When the bus finally arrived, my friend and I took seats on the inside because it was still raining. Not long after, the rain stopped, so we migrated up to the open top section and found seats that were still a little wet. We wiped them with our hands as best we could, which was not especially effective, and sat down anyway.

The bus rolled past many of Kuala Lumpur's landmarks, the KL Tower, the Petronas Towers, KLCC, and a temple whose name completely escaped me at the time. I hoped the photos would document the experience and also rescue my memory later. The city looked different at night, less like a list of places and more like a series of lit surfaces and brief impressions, towers appearing between buildings, roads shining from the rain, everything a little reflective and overdone in a way that somehow worked.

After the bus tour, we walked around central KL for a while and ended up in the middle of live music. Actually, two different bands were playing at separate intersections, and their drums and songs drifted across the roads and bounced off the buildings, pulling us further into the crowd almost by accident. It gave the whole area a loose, improvised energy, the kind of thing you find by wandering around after the planned part of the evening is over.

At some point we found a shawarma place and ordered shawarma fries and a soda for takeaway. Yes, takeaway. Not to-go. I am, apparently, assimilating.

Later that night, or more accurately around 2 a.m. the next day, I was back in my apartment with the realization that I had no plans for the weekend and that it was now Friday. This thought was helped along by texts from family and friends back home who very strongly suggested that I should go to Singapore for the weekend.

So I did.

At two in the morning on Friday, I booked a flight and a hotel, then went to sleep. I still had work later that day, but by the end of it I would be on a plane to another new country.
