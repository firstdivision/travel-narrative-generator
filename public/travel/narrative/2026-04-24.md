## Friday, April 24 - Snowman Packing, Thump Thump Departure

After falling asleep at two or three in the morning, I woke up again for work, not entirely sure which number was correct and not entirely convinced it mattered. The whole day had that useless quality workdays get when there is a flight waiting at the far end of them. Once I knew I was going to Singapore later, concentration was basically over. My body was at the desk, but the rest of me had already left.

Eventually the time arrived and I headed back to the apartment to do the final packing. I always tell myself I should be the kind of traveler who packs properly in advance, but apparently that version of me is fictional. What I actually do is lay everything out on a table or the floor and build what I call a "snowman," a system that made more sense back in my skiing days when the analogy first appeared and, for reasons unknown, never left. The idea is simple enough: make sure the snowman has everything he needs for the trip.

It is not a flawless method. Even with the snowman, I have still come disturbingly close to sending him out into the world without underwear.

In the end I shoved everything into my broken roller bag, the one with two damaged wheels that go THUMP THUMP THUMP whenever I try to push it along on the wrong side, which is less a sound than a minor public announcement of poor life management. Then I got into a Grab to KLIA airport Terminal 2, with that particular mix of relief and low-grade chaos that seems to define the start of almost every trip.
