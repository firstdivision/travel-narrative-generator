## April 13 to 17 - Pipelines and Blanket Purchases

```poem
Work built a maze from code and complaint
Rice came in waves, and my greens grew faint
I bought a blue blanket to rescue the night
Then garlic bread closed things out, which felt right
```

Work was work. That was the bargain, really, a small sacrifice in exchange for weekends that could be filled with something new. With a functioning laptop, I was finally able to do real work, which in theory should have been encouraging. Instead I found myself back in the same rabbit hole, the familiar kind that feels unsolvable even while everyone insists it is just one more thing to trace. They are literally called “pipelines,” which would be funny if they did not so often lead nowhere, or worse, back into neat little circles around the metaphorical computing drainpipe.

Still, one of the few universal constants held up. The people you meet are the good part. Just like interstate travel, crossing countries only seemed to prove the same point on a larger scale. By an overwhelming majority, people want the same things: friends, food, family. In my case it was a temporarily adopted work family, but the pattern held. Jokes, stories, the small pleasure of sharing a table, and that very specific kind of collective enthusiasm that appears whenever food is involved.

There were many lunches, including the usual standing Thursday lunch. At some point, though, I started needing greens with a kind of low-grade desperation. Every meal seemed to arrive with rice or noodles. That sounds ideal until it is no longer theoretical and your body begins filing increasingly formal complaints. So I went to Village Grocer for salad fixings, eggs, avocado, and tortilla chips. It might have been culinary homesickness, or maybe just a blunt biological request for fiber. Any kind would do. Preferably all kinds.

Back at the Airbnb, I discovered it was missing what I could only classify as a critical feature: a comfortable blanket. After work I wanted to burrito myself in something soft and substantial, which, now that I think about it, was yet another reference point borrowed from home. So I went to the mall, because they still have those here, and a lot of them, apparently. This one was called DPULZE.

I bought a fuzzy blue blanket, along with hand towels because the bathrooms were also missing those, which felt like another oversight from the same strange school of furnishing. Then I wandered a bit more and inspected this ball. I found a clothing store and bought a nice shirt, the sort of thing that might come in handy later, though I was not yet sure how. There was also a Japanese bakery, which is how I ended up with a flossed chicken roll and some garlic bread.

Not the worst way to patch a day together.
