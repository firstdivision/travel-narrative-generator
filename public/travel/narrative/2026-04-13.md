## Monday April 13 to Friday April 17 - Pipelines, Blankets, Beetles

Work was work. That was the bargain, really, a small sacrifice that let me spend the weekends filling up on new places and new experiences. With an operational laptop, I was finally able to do real work, which in theory sounded promising. In practice, I still found myself down the same never-ending rabbit hole of the unsolvable. They are literally called "pipelines," and more often than not they led to dead ends and long circles around the metaphorical computing drainpipe. Some words in tech are too accurate in the most annoying way.

Still, one of the universal constants held up. The people were the good part.

Just like interstate travel back home, inter-country travel kept proving the same thing by a huge overwhelming majority. Most of us want the same things, friends, food, family. For me, at least for now, that meant a temporarily adopted work family. Jokes, stories, sharing things, and getting excited about food. That part translated perfectly.

There were many lunches, including the standing Thursday lunch, and somewhere in the middle of all the rice and noodles I hit a wall. Every meal I had seemed to come with one or the other. It sounds like a dream until it becomes reality and your body starts quietly filing complaints. I needed greens. Not in an abstract wellness way, just in a very practical, please give me fiber before something goes wrong, kind of way. So I went to Village Grocer and bought salad fixings, eggs, avocado, and tortilla chips. Maybe it was culinary homesickness, maybe just biology. Either way, all kinds of fiber were suddenly welcome.

My Airbnb was also missing a critical feature, the comfy blanket. After work I needed to burrito up in one. Apparently even my comfort instincts come back to food. There was a mall nearby, because they still have those here, and lots of them, it seemed. This one was called DPULZE. I went in with purpose. I bought a fuzzy blue blanket, along with hand towels that were also somehow missing from the bathrooms. Then I inspected this ball, wandered into a clothing store, and bought a nice shirt that might come in handy later. There was also a Japanese bakery where I picked up a flossed chicken roll and a garlic bread, which felt like excellent decision-making at the time.

Back at home, I put salted water on to boil for pasta. It should have been a straightforward dinner. I dumped the remains of a bag of pasta into the pot and immediately saw unexpected black things. Then I saw them in the bag too, and worse, they were moving. Some kind of flower-eating beetle, or at least that was my best guess. Even though I know it probably would have been *fine*, in the broad technical sense, I dumped the whole batch into the trash. No pasta tonight. Like "No Sugar Tonight" by The Guess Who, but with pasta and beetles, which admittedly gives the song less commercial potential.

So dinner became the garlic bread I had bought earlier, plus whatever I could scrounge from my chronically understocked kitchen. At that point, that meant apples, peanut butter, and a blueberry yogurt. Not elegant, but serviceable.

At some point during the week I also went looking for ibuprofen, which turned into one of those minor errands that reminds you you are not at home. In the CVS-like store, I found nothing but shelves and shelves of paracetamol, in other words acetaminophen. I gave up and asked. It turned out ibuprofen was kept in the pharmacy here, meaning behind the counter. It was a small thing, but those are often the things that make a place feel foreign. Toto was not home anymore, and apparently Toto also had a headache.

Dinner options this week also meant more exploration around Tamarind Square. One night I had a fantastic plate of Thai chicken and rice, and it was especially welcome because it was a proper sit-down meal rather than the more casual QR-code-at-the-table routine. The plate came with a whole bone-in chicken thigh, rice obviously, a small salad on the side, and some sort of broth. I was not entirely sure what the broth was for, so I dumped it over the rice, which seemed as good a plan as any. It worked.

Another overdue accomplishment was finally inspecting the rooftop pool in my apartment building. But first, pools need snacks. No, *require* is the right word. That sent me to the little minimart I had only recently discovered on the 10th floor, the one that connects the two towers of the complex. It had the necessities and, more importantly, the non-essentials I was after. I chose one familiar thing, sour cream and onion Pringles, safety and comfort food in a can. Then I picked something new, or at least newly familiar, O'mas Snek Mi snack noodles. The flavor was "Perisa Kari BBQ," which I was fairly certain translated to barbecue curry flavor. At the pool, between cooling swims and staring out past the infinity edge from the 40th floor, both the noodles and the Pringles quietly disappeared a handful at a time.

My final mission of the week was to recreate the ginger coconut drink we had discovered in Puerto Rico, of all places. My Malaysian coworkers helped me look into it a little. The product was made in China, but as far as we could tell, it was not sold anywhere in Malaysia. Business opportunity, probably. More importantly, I was still craving it. So I decided to make my own version.

I bought a bottle of ground ginger, a few different kinds of coconut powder, and some sweetened condensed milk, then went full mad scientist in the under-equipped Airbnb kitchen. What I finally assembled as a recipe was a lot of coconut powder, what seemed like too much, an equally absurd amount of ginger powder, enough that I started wondering how old this ginger powder might be, and two pink plastic Barbie-looking bread knives' worth of condensed milk, because that was the measuring equipment available to me. Then I added hot water and stirred.

It was actually pretty close.

Not the real thing, not quite, but enough to scratch the itch. In a crunch, that was good enough for me.
