## April 13 to 17 - Blankets, Beetles, and Broth

```poem
Work built me pipelines that faithfully circled the drain
I bought greens, a blanket, and carbs to soften my brain
A beetle claimed my pasta, the pool claimed my snacks with cheer
By week's end I brewed fake ginger coconut, and somehow survived here
```

Work was work, which felt like a small sacrifice for the right to spend weekends collecting new experiences. With a functioning laptop, I was at least able to do real work. That did not mean the work itself became any less absurd. I still found myself in the same never-ending rabbit hole of the unsolvable. They are literally called *pipelines*, and more often than not they led to dead ends, loops, and long circles around the metaphorical computing drainpipe.

Still, one of the more reliable constants of travel held up. The people were the good part. Just like interstate travel, inter-country travel kept proving that by an overwhelming majority, we all want the same things, friends, food, family, some version of belonging, even if in my case it was a temporarily adopted work family. There were jokes, stories, shared meals, and the kind of enthusiasm about food that needs no translation.

There were many lunches, including the standing Thursday lunch. But by then I needed greens in a way that felt almost medical. Every meal had involved rice or noodles. That sounds ideal until it becomes your reality for days on end. So I went to Village Grocer for salad fixings, eggs, avocado, and tortilla chips. It may have been culinary homesickness, or maybe just my body putting in a formal request for fiber. Any kind would do. All kinds were preferred.

My Airbnb was also missing a critical feature, a comfy blanket. After work I wanted to burrito myself in one. Yet another reference from home, apparently. So I went to a mall, because they still have those here, and a lot of them. This one was called DPULZE. I bought a fuzzy blue blanket, along with hand towels that were also missing from the bathrooms. Then I wandered a little more, inspecting the place and ending up in a clothing store where I bought a nice shirt that might come in handy. There was also a Japanese bakery, and from there I picked up a flossed chicken roll and a garlic bread.

Back at home, I put on salted pasta water and dumped in the remains of a bag of pasta. Then I noticed unexpected black things in the water. I checked the bag and saw more of them, and they were moving. Some sort of flower-eating beetle, I think. Even knowing it was probably technically fine did not help at all. I dumped the whole pot into the trash. No pasta tonight, apparently. Like "No Sugar Tonight" by The Guess Who, except less sugar and more beetles.

So dinner became the garlic bread I had bought earlier, plus whatever I could scrounge from my chronically understocked kitchen, which at that point seemed to be apples, peanut butter, and a blueberry yogurt.

At some point I also went looking for ibuprofen, which turned into one of those small, mundane errands that reminds you that you are not at home. In the CVS-like store, I found shelf after shelf of paracetamol, in other words acetaminophen, and no ibuprofen at all. I eventually gave up and asked. Here, ibuprofen was in the pharmacy, which meant behind the counter. It was a tiny logistical difference, but enough to make me think, yes, Toto is not home anymore.

Other dinners that week involved more exploration of the Tamarind Square restaurants. One of them turned out a fantastic plate of Thai chicken and rice. It was a welcome sit-down meal, especially because it did not require scanning a QR code at the table and negotiating dinner through my phone first. The plate came with a whole bone-in chicken thigh, rice of course, a small salad on the side, and some sort of broth that I poured over the rice because I was not entirely sure what else I was meant to do with it. It worked.

Another accomplishment of the week was finally inspecting the rooftop pool in the apartment building. But first, pools need snacks. No, *require* is the right word. That gave me an excuse to check out the little minimart I had only recently discovered on the 10th floor, connecting the two towers of the complex. It had both the necessities and the very specific non-essentials I was looking for. I chose one familiar item, sour cream and onion Pringles, the comfort-food option, and one less familiar one, O'mas Snek Mi. The noodles were labeled *Perisa Kari BBQ*, which I was fairly sure translated to barbecue curry flavor. At the pool, between cooling swims and staring out past the edge of the infinity, both the noodles and the Pringles steadily disappeared.

My final mission for the week was to recreate the ginger coconut drink we had discovered in Puerto Rico, of all places. My Malaysian coworkers helped me investigate it a little. It was made in China, but as far as we could tell, it was not sold anywhere in Malaysia. Business opportunity, probably. But I was less interested in the market gap than in satisfying the craving, so I decided to make it myself.

I bought a bottle of ground ginger, a few different kinds of coconut powder, and some sweetened condensed milk, then got to work like a low-budget kitchen scientist. The recipe I assembled was not elegant. It involved a lot of coconut powder, what felt like too much, an equally absurd amount of ginger powder, enough that I started wondering how old the ginger powder might be, and two pink plastic Barbie-looking bread knives' worth of condensed milk, because that was the closest thing this woefully understocked Airbnb kitchen had to a proper measuring tool. Then I added hot water and stirred.

It was actually pretty close.

Not the real thing, obviously, but close enough to scratch the itch, which by the end of the week felt like a legitimate achievement.
