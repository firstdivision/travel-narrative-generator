## Wednesday April 8 - Curry Before Code

I woke early again, mostly because I had gone to sleep early the night before. It seemed like a sensible time to finally find the fitness center in the building and go for a run, one of those small acts that makes a day feel briefly under control before everything turns back into screens and logistics.

Breakfast was another visit to Botanist Café, where I continued my effort to accept that breakfast here did not need to resemble breakfast as I normally understood it. This time it was chicken curry with beans and the same toasted focaccia bread I’d had before, sturdy enough to tear apart and use for dipping. It was very, very good, rich and savory in a way that made the whole idea of eggs feel unnecessarily limiting.

From there I walked over to Celsius Coffee, which was quickly becoming another favorite. I ordered an iced latte to go, along with a grab, and made my way to work.

Work itself was mostly another day of setup. I spent it getting my laptop and development tools in order, reading and responding to emails, answering Teams messages, all the low-drama but time-consuming tasks that make up the scaffolding of actually starting a job somewhere new. Not exciting, exactly, but necessary.

In the afternoon I headed back to my apartment, though not before stopping at Village Grocer for some basic pasta and sauce. I wanted to make an effort not to eat every single meal at a restaurant, tempting as that was beginning to seem. There is a point in travel, or maybe in temporary living, where buying pasta starts to feel like an attempt at moral seriousness.

So I cooked, ate, cleaned, and slept.

A very ordinary ending, which honestly felt pretty good.
