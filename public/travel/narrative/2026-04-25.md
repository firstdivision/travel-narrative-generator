## Saturday, April 25 - Kaya Toast and Surrender

I stumbled out of my room at the Shelton early with my backpack, full of water and my camera, and went off in search of food. It was that particular kind of morning where I was awake enough to be moving but not quite awake enough to have a plan. I wandered around for a while without finding much open, which always feels faintly unreasonable when you're the hungry one, as if a city should have anticipated your timing.

Eventually I came across a place called Ya Kun Kaya Toast. The outside seats were full, and there was a line snaking into the place where you ordered. In my experience, that is usually a good sign, or at least a better sign than an empty dining room at breakfast, so I joined the line with everyone else. I had also heard that this whole toast-box breakfast situation was something I was supposed to do, and by then I was hungry enough to stop resisting the obvious.

Ordering was a little clumsy at first for all the predictable reasons: language barriers, my own unfamiliarity with the process, and the slightly unorganized energy that seems to come with a busy breakfast place doing exactly what it does every day whether you understand it or not. I stood there trying to look like I knew what I was doing, which I did not. After staring over the menu, I settled on what seemed like the default, most popular order: toast, an egg, and coffee.

One thing surprised me. They never asked how I wanted my eggs cooked. So, in true traveler fashion, and following the example of the people ahead of me, I decided not to interfere with fate and just see what showed up.
