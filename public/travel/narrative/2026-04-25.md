## Saturday, April 25 - Kaya Toast and Blind Faith

I stumbled out of my room at the Shelton early with my backpack, topped up on water, camera packed, and a fairly basic mission of finding breakfast. It turned out to be less straightforward than expected. I wandered around for a while without much luck, passing the half-awake version of the city where things looked open from a distance and then very much were not. Hunger has a way of making a short search feel oddly personal.

Eventually I came across Ya Kun Kaya Toast. The seats outside were full, and there was a line feeding into the ordering area inside. In my experience, a line at breakfast is usually the closest thing there is to reliable public testimony, so I joined it. I had also already heard that this toast-and-eggs breakfast situation was something I was supposed to try, which made the decision easier.

Ordering was a little clumsy at first for all the usual reasons: language barriers, my own unfamiliarity with the process, and the loose, slightly chaotic rhythm that seems to settle over a busy breakfast place when everyone already knows what they want except you. After studying the menu longer than was probably necessary, I went with what seemed like the default, most popular order: toast, egg, and coffee.

One thing surprised me. Nobody asked how I wanted my eggs cooked. At that point, in true traveler fashion, I took my cue from the people ahead of me and let it go. Part of being on the road is occasionally handing your breakfast over to fate and seeing what arrives.
