## Saturday, April 25 - Kaya Toast and Surrender

```poem
I went out hungry with a camera and a plan,
Found a breakfast line and joined it like a fan,
The eggs arrived however fate declared them due,
Travel, apparently, is letting breakfast happen to you.
```

I stumbled out of my hotel room at the Shelton early, backpack on, water filled, camera packed, and with the fairly basic ambition of finding food. It seemed simple enough. Then I wandered around for a while and discovered that simple ambitions can still require effort when not much is open and you're carrying your day on your back.

Eventually I came upon Ya Kun Kaya Toast. The outside seats were full, and there was a line leading into the place where you ordered. In my book, that is usually a good sign, or at least a sign that other people know something I don't, which is often good enough. I joined the line.

I had also heard that this toast-box breakfast was something I needed to do, one of those local routines that becomes a small private mission once someone mentions it. So there I was, standing with everyone else, slightly tired, slightly hungry, and already willing to trust the crowd.

Ordering was a little clumsy at first, for all the usual reasons: language barriers, my unfamiliarity with the process, and the mildly chaotic, entirely expected rhythm of a busy breakfast place. Nothing dramatic, just that travel moment where everybody else seems to understand the invisible system and you are trying not to look like you've accidentally wandered behind the scenes.

After staring at the menu long enough to pretend I knew what I was doing, I settled on what seemed like the default, most popular order: toast with an egg and coffee.

One thing surprised me. They never asked how I wanted my eggs cooked. In true traveler fashion, and following the example of the people ahead of me, I decided not to complicate things. In the spirit of food and mild surrender, I figured I would just wait and see what showed up.
