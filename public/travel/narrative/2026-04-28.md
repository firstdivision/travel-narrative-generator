```poem
My old suitcase limped through duty and heat,
So I went to a mall to admit defeat.
Four clerks sold me zippers like matters of state,
Then chicken and broth helped me tolerate fate.
```

## Tuesday, April 28 - Luggage Triage

Tonight we headed to a mall because I needed to buy new luggage to replace the wounded one I had now. It had reached that stage where a suitcase stops feeling like a trusty travel companion and starts feeling like a liability with wheels.

We found a store with a lot of options, including Malaysian brands, which seemed like the appropriate thing to do. Once I showed even mild interest, the staff multiplied. Soon there were four employees involved, bringing suitcase after suitcase out to demonstrate the virtues of each one as if I were making a serious strategic purchase instead of just trying to stop my belongings from escaping in transit.

This one has a front zipper. This one is also very nice.

That was more or less the sales pitch, repeated with cheerful conviction across an expanding parade of luggage. I was slightly tired, slightly overwhelmed, and still very capable of having opinions about wheel smoothness and shell texture. Eventually I settled on one. I liked the new hardcase style of the carry-on, and there was something reassuring about its clean, solid shape. It looked ready for more than the old one did. At that point, so was I.

Dinner was in the mall food court, which felt right for the evening. Nothing romantic, just practical hunger in bright lighting. I had chicken rice and broth. It was good, warm and straightforward, the kind of meal that lands gently after a day of moving around. Still, the memory of the crispy chicken in the Singapore airport overshadowed it. An unfair comparison, probably, but travel has a way of making certain meals absurdly memorable, and everything after them has to live in their shadow for a while.
