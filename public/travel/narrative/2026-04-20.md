## Monday, April 20 - Plastic Cutlery Monday

```poem
Monday arrived with chicken and rice in practical disguise
A lunch line shuffled forward beneath the noon-day skies
I fenced with a drumstick using cutlery built to fail
And somehow still ate well, which felt like a modest tale
```

At the beginning of the workweek, I joined one of my coworkers for lunch at the spot next door. It had the kind of line that looked mostly permanent, as if it might still be there after sunset, a standing arrangement between office workers and a cluster of food trucks or food stalls set up for the daily rush.

We both ordered the same thing, some sort of bone-in chicken with rice, though I forgot the name almost immediately and was fairly sure I had not pronounced it correctly when I ordered it. That seemed fine. The food arrived anyway.

It came with plastic spoons and knives, which is one of those small adjustments you have to make without much ceremony. There is a particular skill to getting meat off a chicken thigh bone with bendy, undersized, fragile utensils that seem engineered to lose confidence halfway through the job. By now I was getting used to it, or at least pretending to.
