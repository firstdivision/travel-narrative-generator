## Thursday, April 23 - Rain, Rice, and Impulse

I went to work, then had lunch with the crew at a new restaurant called India Portal. I ordered a coffee drink whose name I of course cannot remember, but it was definitely made with cardamom, which is one of the best spices in the world. Anybody who knows me knows my family makes cardamom bread, and when one person was first handed a fresh warm loaf by my mother, his reaction was, essentially, that it was the best bread he had ever had in his life. That still feels like a fair review.

At the restaurant I also ordered a braised lamb shank, which came with two kinds of rice. For some reason I gladly accepted this, even though I had been getting a little tired of rice by this point, since so many things I had eaten came with rice or were almost entirely about rice. This dish was no exception. The piles of rice dwarfed the single lamb shank on the plate, and try as I might, I could not finish either the white rice or the biryani rice. Still, it was excellent, especially the lamb shank, tender and rich with a dark sauce that matched the rice very well.

Later that night I met up with my new friend to go on a Kuala Lumpur night bus tour. It was the same bus that normally runs during the day for the hop-on hop-off city tours, but at night it becomes a roughly two-hour drive past many of the highlights of the city, with two stops where you can get off, walk around, and take pictures.

About twenty minutes before the bus was supposed to arrive, a tremendously loud crack peeled across the sky. That could only mean one thing, thunder and rain were coming. Sure enough, the storm arrived almost immediately, pouring down in torrential sheets that turned the road into a river. Everybody waiting for the bus crowded under an overhang, pressed together and watching the water hammer the street. And of course, despite the weather, several of us still darted out into the torrent to take a quick video for social media. Apparently torrential rain does not stop anyone from posting. If anything, it improves the content.

When the bus arrived, my friend and I managed to get seats on the inside because it was still raining. Soon enough the rain stopped, and we migrated upstairs to the open top section, where we found seats that were still slightly wet. We wiped them down as best we could with our hands and sat anyway.

The bus rolled past many of Kuala Lumpur’s landmarks, the KL Tower, the Petronas Towers, KLCC, and a temple whose name I was completely forgetting at the time and hoped my photos would later remind me of. At night the city had that polished, slightly unreal look that big downtowns get when the buildings are lit from below and the roads are still glossy from rain.

After the tour, we spent some time walking around central KL and wandered into live music. Actually, two different bands were playing at separate intersections, and the music and drums drifted across the roads and bounced off the buildings in a way that kept pulling us farther along. We followed the sound into the crowd, then later found a shawarma place and ordered shawarma fries and a soda for takeaway. Yes, takeaway. Not to go. Another small linguistic adjustment.

Later that night, or really the next day at around 2 a.m., I decided that since I had no plans for the weekend and it was now Friday, I had better make some. This decision was helped along by texts from family and friends back home, who very strongly suggested that I go to Singapore for the weekend.

So I did.

I booked a flight and a hotel at two in the morning on Friday, then went to sleep for a few hours before getting ready for work.
