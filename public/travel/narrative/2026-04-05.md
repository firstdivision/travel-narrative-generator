## Friday, April 5

The light came up and the shades went up with it. There was water down below. It looked like any other ocean view from 30,000 feet, but I knew it wasn't. This was JAPAN ocean.

I had chosen to fly into Narita intentionally so I could take a train into Tokyo. I had heard it was a great little train ride, and after so many hours in transit, arriving by rail seemed more appealing than being dumped into the city by some anonymous airport transfer.

First, though, I had to make my way to the train while towing my broken roller bag behind me. My carry-on suitcase had decided back in the Austin airport that its two front wheels were done participating in this journey. Pulling it the normal way now produced an irritating THUMP THUMP as the broken wheel treads tried and failed to behave like wheels. There was something almost offensive about the timing. My supposedly trusty carry-on chose the dawn of a two-month trip to begin its dramatic exit from the world.

I found the signs pointing to the trains. They were in English too, which felt like one of those privileges Americans are so used to that we barely notice it, until we're standing in another country being quietly accommodated.

Then I got lost anyway.

I backtracked to the trusty information booth, where a Japanese man explained in perfect English that I needed to go to the office with the big "JR" sign over it. Japan Rail. Simple, once someone told me.

At the JR office I talked to another very nice, very polite woman. I was starting to notice a theme. She sold me a ticket for the next NRT Express, a nearly direct train that would take me to Shinjuku Station, my next stop.

The train arrived on time, exactly as expected. Someone once made a whole slogan out of keeping the trains running on time. Whoever it was, the basic point seemed to hold here.

Inside, the train was very clean and very quiet. No fuss, no clutter, no drama, which was more than I could say for my luggage. I spent the ride looking out the window through a haze of jet lag and the particular kind of travel exhaustion that comes from crossing many thousands of miles. I was too tired to form any grand conclusions. Mostly I just watched Japan arrive slowly, through the glass, while my brain struggled to catch up with the fact that I was actually there.
