## Tuesday April 7 - Too Early, Then Setup

I woke up earlier than I needed to for work, which felt like a pointless little betrayal by my own brain. There was no reason for it. I was supposed to meet someone at work in the afternoon, but once I was awake and stuck with it, I realized I might as well go pick up my laptop in the morning instead.

It made practical sense, at least. If I got the laptop early, I could spend the rest of the day setting it up, and that always takes a while. There is no glamorous version of this. Just the slow, slightly numbing process of turning a new machine into *my* machine, clicking through settings, signing into things, remembering passwords I should probably know by now, and waiting for everything to behave.

So the day shifted shape. What had been an afternoon obligation became a morning errand, then a long stretch of setup. It was useful, even satisfying in a narrow way, but mostly it was one of those days governed by logistics and mild annoyance, beginning with waking up too early for no good reason and continuing in the same practical spirit from there.
