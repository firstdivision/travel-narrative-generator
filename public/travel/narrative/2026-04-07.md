## Tuesday April 7 - Badge, Gate, Laptop

```poem
My brain rang dawn's alarm with no permission slip
A guarded gate then stopped my very simple trip
One badge in Texas turned a lobby into art
By dusk I’d set up Windows and misplaced my heart
```

I woke up earlier than I needed to for work, which felt less like discipline and more like my brain being stupid on purpose. I had been supposed to meet someone at work in the afternoon, but once I was awake and functional enough, it occurred to me that I might as well go pick up my laptop in the morning. Then I could spend the rest of the day setting it up, which always takes longer than it should, no matter what anyone claims.

That plan held together right up until my Grab pulled up outside the office and I was reminded that offices, especially large corporate ones, are never content to let a simple errand remain simple. There was a gate. Inside the gate was a security officer. And the security officer would not let me in without my badge, which I had not realized was still back in Austin, Texas. Not ideal.

He asked for my badge number. So there I was, standing outside the office on my phone, digging through far too many Teams conversations until I found one where I had sent the number to a coworker. I gave it to the guy at the gate. He looked me up in the system, then held up his phone and showed me a picture of myself.

Yeah, that was me. I just had longer hair back then.

Apparently that counted as authentication. The Grab and I were finally allowed into the super secure secret parking lot, and I got dropped off in front of the building. Naturally, this did not mean I was actually *in* the building in any meaningful sense. My first mission, which had been to pick up my laptop, immediately became something more basic: how exactly was I supposed to get past the main lobby?

The security guards there informed me, with the kind of calm that suggests they were about to hand me a problem and keep none of it for themselves, that this was going to be an issue. They did not think I could do that here. I could not get a new badge here.

After more back-and-forth, and the usual slow crawl toward the one useful piece of information in the room, they remembered that I needed to email the security office. If I explained the problem there, then, apparently, they would be able to print me a new badge. Good to know, eventually.

I knew my laptop was on the fourth floor, being loaned to me while I was in Malaysia, so I went up there. Thankfully, someone was sitting outside the secure doors. After a conversation with her, she was able to escort me into the tech office, which felt less like success than a temporary clerical exception, but I was in. They handed me the laptop, and I started the long, slightly numbing process of setting everything up.

Which basically meant setting up Windows from scratch, so really, everything.
