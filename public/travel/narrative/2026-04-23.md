## Thursday, April 23 - Cardamom, Rain, and a 2 A.M. Mistake

```poem
I followed the spice and surrendered to rice,
Got rained on at dusk, which felt oddly nice,
Rode Kuala Lumpur shiny, soaked, and bright,
Then booked Singapore half-asleep at two in the night
```

I went to work, and at lunch the work crew tried a new restaurant called India Portal. I ordered a coffee drink whose name I have already lost, which feels consistent with how I operate, but it was definitely made with cardamom, and that was enough for me. Cardamom is possibly one of the best spices in the world. Anybody who knows me knows my family makes cardamom bread, and when people taste it, the reaction tends to be immediate and not particularly restrained. One person, after my mother handed him a fresh warm loaf, announced that it was the best [expletive] bread he’d ever had in his life. I still think that was a fair assessment.

At the restaurant, I also ordered a braised lamb shank that came with two kinds of rice. For some reason, I accepted this arrangement gladly, despite the fact that I’d already started getting a little tired of rice, which appears beside nearly everything here and often seems to be the main event whether you asked for that or not. This plate was no exception. The lamb shank sat there looking noble but outnumbered, dwarfed by mounds of white rice and biryani rice. I did what I could, but there was no realistic path to finishing both piles. Still, the dish was excellent, especially the lamb, tender enough to fall apart under the fork, with a dark rich sauce that matched the rice perfectly. And the coffee, with cardamom and whatever other spices were in it, completely stole my heart.

Later that night I met up with my new friend to do a Kuala Lumpur night bus tour. It was the same bus used for the daytime hop-on hop-off city tours, except at night it became a roughly two-hour loop past the city’s major highlights, with two stops where everyone could get off, walk around, and take photos.

About twenty minutes before the bus was supposed to arrive, there was a crack across the sky so loud and sharp that it felt less like weather and more like a warning. Thunder. Then rain. We were not disappointed. The storm came down in hard, slanted sheets that turned the road into something close to a river. Everyone waiting for the bus crowded under an overhang. Every so often one of us would make a run into the torrent to take a quick video. Not even torrential rain, apparently, can stop people from posting to the socials. If anything, it may improve the content.

When the bus arrived, my friend and I took seats inside because it was still pouring. But the rain eased soon after, and we migrated upstairs to the open top section, where we found seats that were, yes, still a little wet. We wiped them off with our hands in a gesture of optimism more than effectiveness and sat down anyway.

The ride took us past so many of Kuala Lumpur’s landmarks, all lit up and performing exactly as a city should at night. The KL Tower. The Petronas Towers. KLCC. A temple whose name I completely forgot, which is annoying, but hopefully I took enough pictures to document both the experience and the answer to my own forgetfulness. From the top deck, everything felt a little theatrical in the best way, the city all glass and light and damp air after the storm.

After the tour, we walked around Central KL for a while and ended up in the middle of live music. Two different bands, actually, each set up near intersections, with drums and voices drifting across the roads and bouncing off the buildings. The sound pulled us forward. We kept walking deeper into the crowd just to see where it was coming from.

At some point we found a shawarma place and ordered shawarma fries and a soda for takeaway. Yes, takeaway. Not to-go. I’m assimilating.

Later that night, or more accurately the next day at around 2 a.m., I was back in my apartment and realized that since it was now Friday and I had no plans for the weekend, I should probably fix that. This decision was helped along by texts from family and friends back home who strongly suggested that I should go to Singapore for the weekend. So, in a move that felt either adventurous or sleep-deprived, I did.

I booked a flight and a hotel at two in the morning on Friday and then went to sleep. I still had to work later that day, but by the end of it I’d be getting on a plane to another new country.
