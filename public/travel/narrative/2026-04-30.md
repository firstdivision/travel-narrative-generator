## Thursday, April 30 - Curry Nerves and Dinner Fries

At work, my new work friends took me out to lunch, which was a nice kind of generosity that also carried a little social pressure. We went to a restaurant near the work building and ordered the way I always think of as family style, plates and bowls set in the middle of the table so everyone could take what they wanted. It is one of the better ways to eat, assuming you are willing to commit to a bit of reaching and a bit of uncertainty.

There were a lot of dishes on the table, but the one that stayed with me was a yellow spicy curry. I never figured out exactly what it was called. A little Googling later suggested it might have been Peranakan-style curry, which sounded plausible enough to file away as a maybe. Whatever the proper name was, I liked it a lot. It had that kind of heat that made itself known immediately but didn’t ruin the rest of the meal, just sat there and insisted on being noticed. I think I may have earned a few points for handling the spice without embarrassment, or at least without visible collapse.

Later, after work, my friend and I met up near where I was staying and went out again for dinner at a nearby restaurant. By that point the day had folded into that slightly blurred evening feeling where I was still happy to be out, but also a little tired and very easy to persuade with food. I think I had some variation of another chicken and rice dish, familiar enough to be comforting, the kind of meal that does not ask much of you.

Then I saw a plate of french fries go by.

That was the end of any pretense of restraint. I ordered fries too. It did not feel like a thoughtful culinary decision, and I was fine with that. Sometimes the day gets split cleanly in two: the meal where you prove you can handle the spicy curry, and the meal where you see fries at another table and immediately abandon higher principles.
