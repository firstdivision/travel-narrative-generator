## Thursday, April 9 - Lunch, Greens, Crackers

Yesterday at work, someone had asked if I was hungry and wanted to go to lunch, and I had to turn it down because I had eaten that chicken curry with bread fairly late and just wasn't hungry. Maybe tomorrow, I said. So today I made a point of being ready for it. Breakfast at the apartment was deliberately light, just yogurt, an apple, and an Earl Grey tea without sugar. I still had not been able to find a small bag of sugar anywhere, which felt like a minor but persistent annoyance. Still, I was looking forward to lunch and curious where they might take me.

When lunchtime arrived, the troops gathered and we all piled into one person's car to head to a local Indian restaurant. It was one of those half-inside, half-outside places, open at the front and back, with tables set out in the moving air and a somewhat buffet-style system for getting food. First you decided whether you wanted rice, and if so how much. Once that was settled, they handed over the plate and you moved along to the trays, choosing from the separate meat and non-meat sections.

I went for various curried chickens that looked good in the pans, and also something described as a chicken cutlet. It was not a chicken cutlet in the way I would normally understand that phrase, not anything like the Italian restaurant version. This was a mixture of greens, maybe spinach, I wasn't entirely sure, with shredded chicken and some other ingredients, all breaded and deep-fried. Whatever it technically was, it ended up being one of my favorite things on the plate. Really, all of the chicken dishes were very good. We also all ordered iced honey lemon drinks without sugar, which I was told was something they usually always get.

Then it was back to work, with a full belly and, finally, a fully operational laptop. That combination made the afternoon feel much more manageable. I ended up staying fairly late at the office, trying to get some things done.

When I finally headed back to the apartment, I stopped again at Village Grocer, though this time with a more corrective mission. I was starting to realize that I had been living mostly on carbs, bread and rice, and protein, mostly chicken, and that I probably needed some fiber before my body filed a formal complaint. So I bought salad and vegetables.

Back in the apartment, I made a salad that looked very much like the salads I usually make for myself at home: mixed greens, two hard-boiled eggs cut into four pieces each, some crumbled cheese on top, and a store-bought dressing that had sounded promising, toasted sesame. It was, I have to admit, definitely pretty good.

And then, as tradition dictates, about an hour after eating the salad I was hungry again. So I dug out the pita crackers I had bought, sliced up some cheddar cheese, and carried out another familiar ritual from home, late-night cheese and crackers while watching TV, before finally falling asleep.
