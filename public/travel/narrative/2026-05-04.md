## Monday, May 4 - Dogs, Chicken, and Good Timing

```poem
I ran for exercise and got delayed by a paw,
Packed picnic supplies with no basket by law,
The monkeys inspected, the lizard went to prowl,
And sunset did its job before the sky turned owl.
```

I got dropped off at a park near me that someone had pointed out earlier. Since I already had to get up to move the car out of the garage, I figured I might as well go for a run too. It felt like the practical kind of decision that only exists because you're already awake and upright.

The park turned out to be better than I expected. There were multiple stray dogs wandering around, relaxed and familiar with the place, and I stopped to give a couple of them ear scratches. One of them really did not want me to leave. He kept putting his paw on my foot every time I tried to walk away, which is a surprisingly effective strategy if your target is soft-hearted and only pretending to be disciplined about exercise.

There were also large ponds with paved walkways around them, and even an elevated path over some kind of wetlands along the pond's edge. I ran along those paths, doing laps until I stopped feeling like running, which is usually a more honest metric than mileage. Then I ran back to my Airbnb along the road.

Somewhere on the way home, probably while I was still sweaty and mildly tired, I had the very clear thought that this would be a great place for a sunset picnic.

After work, I texted her and asked if she wanted to meet me at my apartment so we could go back to the same park she had dropped me off at that morning. Before that, I went to Tamarind Square to gather supplies. I picked up a box of Korean fried chicken, then went over to Village Grocer for coconut water, some almond snacks, and bottled water. All of it got crammed into my work backpack, because I did not happen to have the proverbial wicker basket available.

She met me outside my apartment, and we drove over to the same park. This time we parked and walked across the pond to some picnic tables at the water's edge that I had noticed earlier on my run. It felt satisfying, in a small and very immediate way, to return to a place on purpose after first seeing it by accident.

At first it seemed like we had the whole place to ourselves.

Or so we thought.

Not long after unpacking the food, we saw a couple of monkeys walking down the path toward us. She told me to be careful. I was fully prepared to stuff everything back into the bag if I had to, which felt like a poor but realistic anti-monkey strategy. Thankfully they seemed more interested in something happening in the pond on the other side, and they kept moving.

So we got to eat in peace. We munched on fried chicken and drank coconut water while the sun went down over the water in front of us. The whole thing had that rare feeling of working out exactly as hoped, which usually makes me suspicious, but this time I let it happen.

Then a monitor lizard appeared and crossed the grass at the edge of the pond, slipped into the water, and swam toward a patch of lily pads. The entire time, only its head stayed above the surface.

"He must be on his way to hunt," I said.

By then the sun had dropped lower and our food was gone, so we packed up and left. We took the long way around the pond, including the stretch over the metal grates above the wetlands.

"I'm getting scared," she said.

"Don't worry, I'll protect you!" I said, smiling, which is easy to say on a walkway in the dark when your actual qualifications are unclear.

We made it back to the car just as the sky was making its final shift, from yellow to dark red, then quickly toward lavender and blue. Perfect timing. By the time we made the short drive back to my apartment, it was fully dark. The sun really does set faster here at two degrees north latitude.
