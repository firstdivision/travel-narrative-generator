## Thursday, May 7 - Gearboxes, Rice, and Late-Night Logistics

Date night took us to Sup GearBox Utara Putrajaya, which sounded promising even before the food arrived. The specialty there is a bowl of rich beef-bone soup built around a massive marrow-filled leg joint known as a "gearbox." It had been slow-simmered for hours with spices, herbs, and aromatics, and the broth came out deep and savory, slightly peppery, the kind of soup that announces itself before you even manage to sit down properly.

Ordering, unfortunately, did not go as smoothly as either of us had probably hoped. It was all done in Malay, so I obviously did not understand anything either person said. There was some back and forth, the usual vague smile of a person who is trying to be agreeable while understanding absolutely nothing, and eventually we sat down and our two bowls arrived.

Then the confusion made itself clear.

I had asked for noodles with my beef soup, but mine came with rice instead. The restaurant must have insisted on rice. I had been thinking lately that I was getting a little tired of rice and that noodles sounded good, which was why we ordered noodles in the first place. That was why she had specifically asked them to make mine with noodles.

She offered to go get me noodles instead, which was kind, but by that point I was hungry and the bowl in front of me looked pretty good, so I decided it was fine the way it was. And it was.

Mostly.

I think the beef in my soup maybe had not been cooked quite long enough. What I had anticipated as fall-off-the-bone, more or less as advertised, was in parts fairly tough. A few pieces were cooked well and very tender, and those were excellent. If all of it had been like that, this would have been an easy A+ dinner. As it was, it still landed solidly in the very good category. A respectable B, helped a lot by the broth, which had real depth to it and enough pepper to keep every bite interesting.

After the meal, I was given the job of going to pay for our drinks, which for some reason came from and were paid for at a different place. Naturally.

When I got to the counter, the man said, "lapan," the Malay word for eight. We both stared at each other for a while until he realized that I was clearly an idiot who could not even count to ten in the local language. Then he switched tactics and started counting on his fingers. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight!"

Aha.

I handed him a ten ringgit bill, and he gave me two in change. International understanding, achieved through finger math.

On our way out, we also ordered some to go for my two cousins, who would be arriving that night. There was more confusion when we tried to order just broth and noodles. No meat. They did not understand, or possibly pretended not to understand, and gave us meat anyway. Oh well. Sometimes the universe wants you to have beef whether you asked for it or not.

Later that night, back in my AirBnb, my two cousins texted that they were at the airport. Yay. Then almost immediately they texted that they were confused about how this whole Grab thing worked. Oh no. Then another message came through saying they had managed to order a Grab and were in it, but they had entered "pay with cash" and did I perhaps have any?

They finally arrived, and we all went back up to the AirBnb, where I warmed up the beef and noodles for them, though I left out the beef. It was already late, but we decided to power through and finish all of our eVisas for the rest of the trip that night. So that became the rest of the evening: reheated broth, tired people, glowing phone screens, forms, uploads, corrections, and that slightly grim determination that only shows up when everyone is too far in to stop.

It was getting toward one in the morning when we finally finished. Very late for the two locals. Maybe not so much for the newcomers.
