## Thursday, May 7 - Gearbox Soup and Late Arrivals

```poem
I ordered rice though noodles were clearly the play
The soup was good enough to hush my doubts anyway
Then Grab, cash, and cousins rolled in near one
Travel planning at midnight is nobody's fun
```

Date night tonight took us to Sup GearBox Utara Putrajaya, a place built around one very specific idea: a bowl of rich beef-bone soup centered on a massive marrow-filled leg joint they call a "gearbox." The broth had been slow-simmered for hours with spices, herbs, and aromatics until it came out deep, savory, and slightly peppery, the kind of soup that announces itself before you even properly sit down.

She asked me if I wanted rice or noodles, and I said rice. This all happened in a flurry of Malay, so I obviously did not understand much of what either person said. There was some back and forth at the counter, then somehow we were seated, and our two bowls arrived.

I had been thinking lately that I was getting a little tired of rice, so I have no idea why I ordered mine with rice. Maybe my brain just defaulted to some familiar setting without consulting me. Or maybe I did not yet understand what I was ordering. I am still not sure.

I mentioned this, and she offered to go get me noodles instead, but it was fine the way it was, because it was. Also, I was really hungry, and once the bowl was in front of me that debate felt theoretical at best.

The soup itself was very good, though not quite the full fantasy I had built up in my head. I think some of the beef in my bowl had not been cooked quite long enough. What I had expected to be fully falling apart, as advertised, was in places fairly tough. Still, a few pieces were exactly what I had wanted, soft and tender and easy, and those bites made the whole thing make sense. If all of it had been like that, this would have been an A+ dinner. As it was, I would call it a solid B, which is still a good place to be when the broth is rich and hot and I am already halfway committed to forgiving everything.

After the meal, I was given the job of going to pay for our drinks, which for some reason came from and were paid for at a different place. Naturally. When I got to the counter, the man said, "lapan," the Malay word for eight. We then stared at each other for a while until he realized that I clearly am an idiot and cannot even count to ten in the local language.

So he started counting on his fingers. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight!"

Aha.

I handed him a ten ringgit bill, and he gave me two back. International commerce survived once again.

On our way out, we also ordered some to go for my two cousins, who would be arriving that night. There was some additional confusion because we wanted just broth and noodles, no meat. They did not understand, or perhaps pretended not to understand, and gave us meat anyway. Oh well. The system had spoken.

Later that night, back in my Airbnb, my two cousins texted that they were at the airport. Yay. Then came the next part: they were confused about how this whole Grab thing works. Oh no. Then another text arrived saying they had managed to order a Grab and were already in it, but they had selected pay with cash and did I perhaps have any?

They finally arrived, and we all went back up to the Airbnb, where I warmed up the beef and noodles for them, leaving out the beef. It was already late, but we decided to power through and finish all of our eVisas for the rest of the trip that night. By the time we were done, it was getting toward one in the morning. Very late for the two locals, maybe not so much for the newcomers.
