## Saturday, April 25 

Stumbled out of my hotel room at the Shelton early with my backpack. Filled up with water and my camera and search of food. After wondering around a while and not finding much open. I came up on a place called Ya Kun Kaya Toast. The seats outside were full. And there was a line leading in to the place where you ordered. And in my book. That is always a good sign so I decided to join everybody else in line. Also, I had heard that this toast box breakfast was something I needed to partake in. 
Ordering was a little clumsy at first, from any reasons, language, barriers, my unfamiliarity with the process, and the somewhat expected unorganized nature of a busy breakfast restaurant. After looking over the menu, I decided on what seemed like the default, most popular order of toast with an egg and coffee. 

One thing. I was surprised that was that they did not ask me how I wanted. My eggs cooked so true traveler fashion. In the example of those before me. In the adventure of food I decided I'll just see what shows up.

Afterwards I went back to the hotel to regroup, and when I left the hotel a hop-on hop-off bus was just pulling up.  Kismet? I think so.  I buy a ticket and get on.

I plug in my little red headphone set into the jack on the side of the bus and listen to the history of these streets as we drive by.

Looking at the map I see an upcoing stop is Marina Boulavard.  Mandatory stop just based on the name.

Another case if kismet as, while wanding around, I come upon the "Merlion" -- a famous sight to see in Singapore.  Checkbox checked!

I go to get my Nikon camera out of my bag and is is HOT! Like it has been sitting in the summer sun for an hour.  I realize that I left it on, in video mode, and that it has been recording for quite a long time while stuffed inside a bag with no way to dissapate heat.  The battery is nearly dead.  I panic for a moment wondering where the spare was until I remember that, like a good photographer, I brought it with me instead of leaving it back at the hotel!  Crisis averted.  The adventure may continue.

I wander around the water's edge more.  I find a large grass park by the water, but this is fresh water and I know I am close to salt water, I wnt to try to find it. I move on.

Along the way I find a bike stand.  On one side are the new batery-powered rental bikes.  On the other side are old bikes.  Maybe even forgotten.  Rusty, but still in working order.  I pause and image a photograph, or some sort of artwork, trying to depic this relationship.  Old and new.  

Further around this waterfront I get to the main Marina Bay Waterfront Promenade.  I enter the doors of a shopping mall and feel the sweet breath of cold air conditiong waft over me.

I see a coffee shop called "Ralph's" and decide that sounds good.  I order one and sit down in the "outside" seating so I can people-watch as people go about their day in this mall.

An older couple sits down next to me.  I pretend to write in my journal but I am really listening to them:

The older woman takes a sip of her coffee and exlaims, "Oh! That's a mighty fine cup of coffee!"

The man agrees, and says "This is lovely."

"So much better than walking around in the heat." she says in return.

The man pauses, "Not being rushed."

I love this little exchange.  I am imagining them as being on a "singles" tour.  They seemed to not be very familiar with each other.  Maybe the tour was supposed to go see the entire city in one day and they met, decided they maybe didn't want to do the tour after all, and bailed.  

I look at the clock and realie it is already 2PM!  This day is flying by and Im sitting here sitting coffee. I'm the one who needs to be rushing! But I am in that same spot the older couple was in, I am loving this air conditioning.  Too bad - let's go!

I leave the mall, head down some stairs, and find myself along a river promenade, but with funny gates set up.  I think maybe the are to keep bikes away from people? Not sure.

I walk more and finally find one of the things I've been looking to see: Gardens by the Bay.

I buy a ticket for both the Flower Dome and the Cloud Dome, which it says includes "Jurrasic Park".

Inside the Flower Dome you are led through a series of exhibits separated by geography, a common botabical garden tactic.  A girl walks by with her significant other, "It smells so nice in here.", she says softly.  Yes, I agree.  Hundreds of different varieties of flowers all blooming at the same time! It reminds me of my grandmother's old greenhouse that always smelled similar.  A combination of fertilizer and flowers.

One especially nice exhibit was the tulips that were on display in center stage. Red, pink, yellow, multi-color.  And fragrent.

After going around a loop and up and down some ramps, you are deposited at the exit.  And upon exiting you are conviently enough also right in front of the entrance to the next area, the Cloud Dome!

Inside the cloud dome there is a spiral path that leads you up a central tower of ferms and vines.  And along the way are animitronic dinosaurs, including T-Rex and Raptors!  It is really well done, the path is wide enough that don't feel rushed, which also means it is wide enough that you can get past people who are in less of a rush than you.

I leave the Cloud Dome and wander outside some more, I find myself in the supertree garden.
