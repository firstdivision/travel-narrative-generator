## Tuesday, April 28

Tonight we head to a mall because I need to buy new luggage to replace my wounded one I have now.

- We find a store that has a lot of options, including Malaysian brands which seems like the appropriate thing to do. The store employees are soon four in number as they bring suitcase after suitcase out to show me the virtures of each one.  "This one has a front zipper", "This one is also very nice", "This one has TWO zippers!".  Eventually I settle on one. 

The store clerks insisted on wheeling the suitcase to the register for me, where they instruxted the cashier to give me a discount! 

I like the new hardcase style of this carryon suitcase.  And I like the four casters on it too. They glide smoothly over the mall's tiled walkways.  Click clack, click clack.  Like a single-car tiny train, and much less embarrasing than the loud noises and ambulations of my old suitcase that announces to the world every where we go that it doesn't want to.

On our way to somewhere in the mall, I am playing with my new suitcase.  Trying to see if I can get it to do a 360 on its wheels. After about my third attempt I get it and she smiles at me, "You are still a child inside?"

"Yes, sometimes I am." I reply. I think it's a good quality.

- Dinner is in the mall food court.  Chicken rice and broth.  It is good, perfectly fine in fact. But the memory of the Crispy Chicken in the Singapore Airport overshadows it.
