## Thursday, April 30

At work I am treated to lunch by my new work friends.  We go to a restaurant near the work buiding and we order a table of food to be shared what I call "family style" -- in other words -- the plates and bowls of food go in the middle, everyone takes what they want. This small group of people was certainly fast becoming something like a foster family to me.  Anf Family Style food is one of my favorite ways to eat at a restaurant.  Especially a new one.  And especially when it's full of food you ahev never had before.  You can try a little bit of everything!

There are many dishes, but my favorite and standout was a yellow spicy curry.  I'm not sure what it was called, but some quick Googling says maybe it was "Peranakan-style curry"? Google and the internet says it is a golden bowl of coconut-rich gravy, fragrant with lemongrass, turmeric, and a blend of spices that announced themselves before the first bite. Beneath the creamy richness was a steady, lingering heat—spicy enough to make itself known, but balanced by the sweetness of coconut milk and the bright citrus notes of lemongrass. Tender chicken simmered until it nearly fell from the bone, soaking up a sauce that was equal parts comforting and fiery, a reminder that Malaysian Peranakan cuisine rarely forces you to choose between flavor and spice. That certainly sounds like what I had.

In any case I liked it a lot! And I think maybe earned some bonus points for being able to take the heat. That happens a few times on this entire trip, many people are surprised that I do not reach for the nearest water and chug it down, only to ask for more.

As it happens, we ordered a bit too much food for our Family Style meal, so we called in a reinforcement.  Another friend from work arrived shortly and was able to help us finish most of what we ordered.

Later that night, my friend and I meet up near where I am staying, and have dinner at a nearby restaurant. It is one I have been to before, but it still takes me a while to find it in the concrete maze of Tamarind Square.

I think what we ordered was a garlic rice and chicken dish?  I don't quite remember, but I know I liked the restaurant.  It was quiet which is sometimes hard to find.  I mentioned to her that this is close to the kind of "sit down" restaurant I had in mind when I mentioned that fact sometime earler. It wasn't *exactly* what I had in mind.  What I wanted was to take her somewhere *really nice*, but I think places like that ar emuch easier to find in downtown Kuala Lumpur, and not so much in Tamarind Square.

Oh, I nearly forgot.  While ordering our meal (you have to order at the counter), on the walk back I saw some french fries being delieverd to a table.

"I think I'm going to order Fries too", I say.

"Will you be able to eat it all?", she says.

"I'm not sure. But I think I want them."

A moment of hesitation and then I'm off to the counter again to order a side of fries.

As it turns out, I was *not* able to eat everything.  Even adults sometimes fall victim to their eyes being bigger than their stomachs.
