Angkor Wat Temple is:
Enormous 12th-century temple complex and the largest religious monument in the world.
Built by the Khmer Empire under King Suryavarman II.
Originally a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later transformed into a Buddhist site.
Famous for its towering lotus-shaped spires and huge reflective moat.
Covered in intricate stone carvings showing mythology, battles, apsaras, and scenes of Khmer life.
Designed with remarkable symmetry and symbolism, representing Mount Meru from Hindu cosmology.
Located near Siem Reap within the larger Angkor Archaeological Park.
Sunrise at Angkor Wat is one of the most iconic travel experiences in Southeast Asia.
The temple feels less like a building and more like an ancient stone city rising from the jungle.
Even with crowds, quiet corridors and weathered stone passages still feel mysterious and timeless.
