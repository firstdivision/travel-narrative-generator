# Monday, April 6

- Wake up early, both maybe because of lingering jetlag but also the standard "I'm in a new place and can't sleep".  I never do the first couple days in a new place when travelling.  I think it's the old reptile brain keeping me awake to scan for danger, only when that part of the brain is satisfied that a sabertooth tiger is not going to eat us in the middle of the night does it allow me to really sleep soundly.
- I decide to walk over to "Tamarind Square", which is a shopping center across the street.  First I have to figure out how to get out of my building.  In the lobby floor of the elevator there are double dooes that lead outside, and a sign on the glass doors says "push".  I do.  Nothing happens.  I push harder.  Nothing happens.  Hmmm.....there is a man from the cleaning staff in the lobby.  I turn around and ask him.  He doesn't speak English, but says something and points back to the door.  Ok.  I push even harder and this time both he and one of the guards in the ouside lobby see what I am doing and start yelling at me and pointing.  I stop pushing.  The point to the side of the door where, tucked away without a sign acknowledging its existence, is a button.  Apparently you push that to unlock the door from the inside.  I wonder what happens if the power goes out, or that switch breaks?  Hopfully the door "fails safe" and unlocks itself?  Or maybe is you push really, really hard the door will open.  Or maybe shatter into ten thousand diamond shaped pieces.  Lesson learned.  I know how to open doors now.
- I start to walk out the road entrance to the outside and get yelled at again.  Again a pointed finger diretcs me.  There is another way out.  A path, stones, and anpother gated door that leads out.  It requires my RFID key to open.  Boy.  Lots of doors to learn how to open!
- I make it across the no-crosswalk-here four lane street to the actual Tamarind Square lot.  It has a somewhat-futeristic and brutalist look to it. But also with trees growing from balconies and the roof.  Like it belongs in some apacolytic movie.  War ravged but now civilzation has returned, but not before nature tried to reclaim the conrete.
- In another failure, I cannot find my way into this place.  There are stores and restaurants along the outer road level, most are closed except for the daycares that are very busy with dropoffs at the moment.  It is still early morning afterall.
- Inside one doorway I see an escelator.  It isn't moving and appears to lead up to a dark hallway.  That seems like it *should* be it, but also *cannot* be it.  I move on. I try a stairwell.  It leads me to some Disney-esque behind-the-scenes area where employees of restaurants seem to be preparing for the day.  Well, at least they will be open eventually I think.  I do get one questioning look from a woman as she bustles by on her way to do something, as if to say "What are you doing here?".  I retreat back down the stairs to the road.
- Eventually I find the entrance to a parking garage, and inside there is an elevator with signs on it.  I select level three.  Exiting the elevator I find myself finally on the inside of this place.  There is more roadways with cars parked, and more restaurant and store fronts.  I wander, something I'm pretty good at it seems.  
- Village Grocer is closed until 10:30, but Celsius Coffee rightnext to it is not.  I like coffee, so I go in.  Something I will come to learn about KL is that there are a lot of Coffee Places, and that it's also relatively expensive.  My Rideshares in Grab that I will eventually take to work will average around $2, but a Caffe Latte will run me about $3.  First day in KL and my sense of money is already shot.
- After coffee the grocery still is not open, so I take another walk.  I find a little restaurant tucked away inside a labrintyan setof stairs, and hanging greenery.  Birds a chirping, and the tables are both inside an outside somehow.  It's perfect.  I sit, scan the QR code menu, and order from my phone.  I have a drink made from carrot, pineappe, and orange juice - amazing.  And a simple plate of toased bread with jam and butter, the good kind of butter, full fat and salted.  It makes me think of similar breakfasts in Paris.  In the morning when it is quiet like this place is now, you can find that small cafe and order a coffee and bagette with butter and jam.  The same feeling of relaxation and "everything is right with the world" finds me also here in this small cafe set in the middle of a concrete maze, that also enbraces nature within it.  I'm starting to settle in.
- Village Grocer is open now, so I go thee to accpmplish my main mission -- cleaning supplies.  The AirBnb is fine, but it needs some cleaning.  I buy sprays, sponges, microfiber cloths.  All these to replace their very weary peers that are back in the apartment.  
- Return to the apartment.  Clean, and clean some more.  Wash dishes and put them in a special place in the cabinet for "things that I washed and I know are clean.  The same for a slot in the utensils tray.  I clean the butter knife.  Yes, you read that correctly.  THE butter knife, the only one, plastic and pink like it escaped from an adult-sized Barbie's Dream Kitchen playset.
- In the evening I return to Tamarind Square for dinner.  It is much busier now, it has come alive. I wander again, slowly learing my way around this crazy maze of concete, escelators, and stairways to nowhere. 
- I pause to look at the menu of one restaurant.  Many of the restauramt menus I have already seen look the same.  Rice being the unifying factor, the starchy glue that knits nearly meal photogrhaph I see.  A hostess comes out to greet me, her hijab frames her face as she smiles and asks me if I would like to come in to eat.  An effective tactic, it would be rude to say know, right?  I go in and take a seat.  Another QR code scanned and order placed.  Garlic fried rice with ginger chicken, and a "passionfruit mint longan" drink to pair it with.  Both are very good and leave me satisfied.  As I leave all three of the women in the restaurant wave with a smile and a thank you.  I return the gesture.
- Back at the apartment I learn how to stream youtube from my phine to the TV.
- I am more comfortable now.  A full belly and some exploring to do, so I do not sleep on the couch and instead move to the bedroom.  
- Tomorrow will be my first day working in the Malaysia office and I need my rest.
