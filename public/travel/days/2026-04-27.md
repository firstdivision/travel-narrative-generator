## April 27 to May 9

Is this title a little different than the others?  Yes, because it spans many days.

You see, I had stopped journaling during this time.  But it's for a very good reason.  The "new friend" who I alluded to earlier, we starting hanging out and going to places every night after work more and more.  I found myself with neither the time, nor the real desire to write things down. I rather wanted to spend it all with her.  So this is a "mega post" of that time:

**Monday, April 27**

It is Monday, my first day back from Singapore.  I work, of course. I'm sure there was a breakfast, but I don't remember what it was.  Or perhaps I was too tired for that and instead got a latte and Grab to work?

After work though, we go to a park nearby. It's a nice park with wide curved pathways perfect for walking slow, talking, and making up stories. 

It is sunset, and we take turns finding more sunset pictures to take, while also asking each other questions about each other.

After meeting a cat we made up a story about two people who get locked into the park because they lost track of time and the gates are closed - they are unable to leave:

    The Magic Tree in the Park:

    They couple find a tree and crawl under it for shelter as the sun goes down.  But then they hear a noise, startled and scared at first, they soon see it is the cat they had met earlier.  They are not frightened any more but are soon startled when the cat speaks to them!

    "Thank you for the pets earlier! They were magnificent!" the cat says.

    Still too startled to talk the couple stares at the cat in disbelief.  Then another animal arrives.  A squirrel! And this one talks too!

    "Oh cat! Leave those two alone!" the squirrel says.

    "I am not bothering them, I was thanking them!" cat replies.

    No longer scared the couple talks with the animals all night long.  An owl, a tiny ant, a moth who flutters by and stays a while to talk, before flying away again.

    The sun rises, and the couple who had fallen asleep sometime late into the night wake up to the cat and squirrel are sleeping next to them.  The cat softly purring even as he sleeps.  Soon they awake too.

    All say goodbye, but the couple promise to return again.  And they do.  They return many nights, for many years.  Later they bring their kids to meet the animals in the park.  Cat is old now, his hair slightly grey, but he introduces his wife and kittens as well.

    And the story goes on and on.  The magic tree in the park where special people and animals talk to each other and tell each other stories of their lives.

- For us, we are not locked out of the park. We gett into the car and drive home.  Taking pictures of the sunset as we do.

**Friday, May 1st**

Tonight we have a double-header planned.  First is to go to Batu Caves which is one of Malaysia's most famous religious and cultural landmarks, a series of limestone caves and cave temples rising from a hillside just outside Kuala Lumpur. Visitors are greeted by a towering golden statue of the Hindu deity Lord Murugan before climbing the colorful 272-step staircase that leads to the main cathedral cave. Inside, the immense cavern opens toward the sky through natural openings in the rock, creating dramatic shafts of sunlight that illuminate shrines, worshippers, and the occasional troop of mischievous monkeys. More than just a tourist attraction, Batu Caves is an active Hindu pilgrimage site and the focal point of the annual Thaipusam festival, when hundreds of thousands of devotees gather for one of the largest religious celebrations in Southeast Asia.

After we get back in the car and head to Genting Highlands. Along the way my job is to pick new music to play.  

Genting highlands is nice in one way certainly. It is a mountain resort destination perched high in the Titiwangsa Range about an hour from Kuala Lumpur, where cool temperatures and misty forests offer a refreshing escape from Malaysia's tropical heat. Known for its hotels, casinos, shopping, entertainment venues, and indoor and outdoor theme parks, Genting combines the feel of a hill station with that of a bustling resort city. Many visitors arrive via the scenic cable car, which glides over dense rainforest and provides sweeping views of the surrounding mountains before emerging into the clouds at the summit.

We are hungry so our first stop is a Ramen restaurant.  It is Ichiban Ramen.  In the moment I think it is the same place I went to in Tokyo.  But research after the fact shows that the one in Tokyo was "Ichiran".  In any case the ramen is very good, warms us up, and fuels us for the next part of the adventure.

Ater we head directly for the cable car, and are immediately faced with a gigantic line.  You know the kind -- back and forth and back and forth.  What I call a "Disney Line". There are two of these lines, one for tickets, and one for getting on the cable car.  We also discover that there are "VIP" tickets you can buy online, and you get to skip the line.

I LOVE skipping lines whenever possible.  I look up the tickets online, they are not that much more expensive than the regular ones.  Add to cart two tickets please.

We show the QR codes to an attendant.  A red velvet rope is lifted, and we are walking down an empty hallway with windows through which we can see the full line snaking back and forth.  This must be what it feels like to be rich!

The cable car delivers us to the top of the mountain where there are attractions and a mall.

We go inside the mall where the bright neon lights of stores and carinval rides draws a stark contrast to the green misty hills outside.  And yes, you head me right, carnival rides.  Ferris wheel, games, all right in this mountain-top mall!

Inside we play some games.  There is an archery place, and video games where we play Halo in the kind of video game where there is a racecar style seat, large TV screens in front of you, guns that track on the screen.

We also play two games of basketball.  The first game we did ok, sinking twenty-something balls, but the second game we do not do as well.  We chose a different machine to play from and this one had half as many balls in it! I suspect previous players have moved balls from this game to the other one in order to have more to shoot.

With our final bit of change on the plastic game card we can only find a single game that is inexpensive enough to play.  It is a street fighter style game.  It turns out to be the best value! I am able to get through multiple "fights" on my single play.  Finally I get to a level where the opponent clobbers me in each of the three bouts.  I did not stand a chance, but it was money well spent!

After we head back to the cable car "go down" side.  But we stop at a midpoint landing to check out a temple.

Rising from the mist below Genting's casinos and hotels, Chin Swee Caves Temple feels like a different world entirely—a mountainside sanctuary of pagodas, incense, and towering Buddha statues suspended above a sea of rainforest. Reached by cable car or a winding mountain road, it offers some of the most dramatic views in the highlands and a welcome moment of calm amid Genting's constant buzz.

The complex features a towering Buddha statue, a nine-story pagoda, prayer halls, gardens, and sweeping views across the surrounding rainforest-covered mountains.

We take the cable car the rest of the way down to the main area.  Walking to the car I see a candy store and decide to pop in.  It is chocolates of all varieties, including Durian Flavor ones.  I cannot resist buying a few of those for my cousin's when they arrive.  I also get more traditional ones like almond, sea salt, mint.

When leaving it is like leaving a ski resort.  Cars are everywhere as everyone is leaving at the same time. We both admit to each other that we have headaches.  Maybe it was the altitude or just a long day.  Finally, the traffic clears and we make the drive back to Kuala Lumpur which takes a little more than an hour and a half.  

**Sunday, May 3**
I wake early to meet my coworkers in downtown Kuala Lumpur for breakast and then a walkabout. The weekend I went to Singapore I was supposed to meet up with them for a 7am run. I missed that one, but I must admit that the 9am meet time for this breakfast sounds a little easier to make.

We all meet at Luck Bros Kopi. Tucked beside the bustle of Petaling Street, Luck Bros Kopi serves up a polished version of the Malaysian coffee-shop experience. Strong kopi, fragrant nasi lemak, and a menu that hops effortlessly between Chinese, Malay, and Peranakan influences make it feel like a small celebration of the country's food culture under one roof.  I order an egg and tomato dish and a latte.  I do like my lattes!

Afterwards we walk to a nearby temple.  The girls don some loaner robes (or did they have to rent them, I am not sure), and we head inside -- all of us sans shoes. One of the first things I see are more of those prayer saucer things buring oil from a wick I like so much.  I make a mental note to try to find where to buy them. The ceiling of this temple has an especially intracate mosaic that captures my attention.

Around the corner is a Chinese temple - the famous Sri Maha Mariamman Temple in Chinatown.  This one did not allow photographing inside, so I don't have any, but it is pretty cool inside. The temple was founded in 1873 and is the oldest Hindu temple in Kuala Lumpur. Its 22.9-meter-tall entrance tower is decorated with more than 200 sculpted figures of Hindu gods, goddesses, and mythological beings in the traditional Dravidian style of South India. During Thaipusam, it serves as the starting point for the massive pilgrimage procession to Batu Caves.

Afterwards we head towards KLCC mall.  There we stop into a bookstore where I find (but do not buy) a book set priced at 3,561 ringgit.  Even after conversion back to USD this is still a little pricey for me...besides where would I put it?

There is also a large outdoor park we visit.  It is dominated on one side by Pentronas towersm bur has wooded paths for running, water features, and even a small water park for children to play in.

We take a short train ride to TRX where there is another, larger mall.  In here we visit the Apple store where we all look at some Apple watches.  Mine is back in my AirBnB because the battery is dead, but also because Apple Pay does not work on that here for me, which is really what I use it for most of the time back home.

We also go get lunch where there are many things ordered.  A bowl of Ceasar Salad is shared between three of us, a welcome addition of raw vegetables!  Also steak, and some spaghetti.  

Content with full stomachs we all leave and head home.  We do have work tomorrow after all!

The intimate space pairs an impressive selection of Japanese whiskies, highballs, and cocktails with teppanyaki, yakitori, and other Japanese small plates, creating an atmosphere that is equal parts refined restaurant and late-night hideaway. It's the sort of place where the conversation slows down, the whisky pours a little longer, and the city outside seems very far away.

**Wednesday, May 6**

Fresh off the blocks from the "Catfe" we decide tonight to go to a place called "Huskiss" -- the same concept but with Husky dogs!  I am a dog person, her...not so much.  In fact she informs me along the way that she is afraid of dogs!

When we get there I can see she is nervous, and not really happy about the large dogs that are coming up to her to say hello.  But soon enough the dogs win and she is smiling while petting the black and white Husky dogs.  

In a tropical city where the afternoon heat can feel like a wet blanket, Huskiss is an unexpected encounter with the Arctic. For an hour, you're surrounded by a pack of impossibly fluffy huskies who seem equally interested in posing for photos, demanding attention, and sprawling across the floor in search of the next belly rub. It's quirky, a little ridiculous, and impossible to leave without a smile.

But leave we must.  Both because our hour is up and they are closing, but also because we are hungry.  Back onto Google Maps I find a place called Noodle Art and we head in that direction.  Much like many experiences in KL and in South East Asia in general I will find out later, where there is one restaurant there are probably may more.  And that is true here.  However, after making the rounds down the street, and into a parking lot next door where there is a ring of food trucks surrounding tables, we decide on the original idea of Noodle Art SS2.

Noodle Art specializes in the comforting noodle dishes of Sarawak, bringing flavors from East Malaysia to the busy food district of Petaling Jaya. Handmade noodles, rich Sarawak laksa, kolo mee, and other regional specialties take center stage, served in a bright, casual setting that feels more neighborhood favorite than trendy hotspot. For many diners, it's a chance to experience a side of Malaysian cuisine that is less familiar on the peninsula—simple, satisfying dishes built around springy noodles, aromatic broths, and recipes that trace their roots back to Kuching and the towns of Sarawak.

**Thursday, May 7**

Date night tonight takes us to Sub Gearbox Utara Putrajaya.

The specialty at Sup GearBox Utara is a bowl of rich beef-bone soup built around a massive marrow-filled leg joint known as a "gearbox." Slow-simmered for hours with spices, herbs, and aromatics, the broth emerges deep, savory, and slightly peppery, while the tender meat clings to the bone and the soft marrow can be scooped out like nature's butter. Served with lime, chilies, and fresh herbs, it's a gloriously messy meal—part soup, part treasure hunt—where every spoonful seems to reveal another layer of beefy richness.

Ordering does not go as smooth as I think either of us hoped.  It was all done in Malay so I obviously didn't understand anything either person said.  After some back and forth we sit and our two bowls arrive.  The source if the confusion becomes apparent.  I had asked for noodles with my beef soup but this instead came with rice.  I had been hinting lately that I was getting tired of rice, which was why we ordered noodles.  She asked and offered to go get me noodles instead, but I decided it was fine the way it was.  And besides, I was hungry and this looked prety good.

I think the beef that I got in my soup maybe had not been cooked quite long enough.  What I had anticipated was going to be "fall off the bone" (as advertised) was in parts fairly tough.  A few pieces were cooked well and were very tender, and those were good.  So if it had all been like that then this would have been an A+ dinner.  It was still very good though, let's say a solid B.

After the meal I am given the job of going to pay for our drinks, which for some reason came from and were paid for at a different place.  When I arrived the man at the counter said "lapan" (the Malay word for 8).  We both stared at each other for a while until he realized that I clearly am an idiot and cannot even count to ten in the local language.

He instead starts counting on his fingers, "one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight!"  Aha!  I pass him a ten Ringgit bill and he hands me two in change.  

On our way out we also order some to go for my two cousins who will be arriving tonight.  There is some additional confusion as we order just broth and noodles.  No meat.  They do not (or perhaps pretend not to) understand, and give us meat anyway.  Oh well.

Later on that night, back in My AirBnb my two cousin's text that they are in the airport! Yay! And they are confused how this whole grab thing works.  Oh no!  They then send another text, that they were able to order a Grab and they are in it, but that they entered "pay with cash" and do I perhaps have any?

They finally arrive, and we all go back up the the AirBnb where I warm up the beef and noodles for them (I leave out the beef).  It is already late but we decide to power through and finish all of our eVisas for the rest of the trip tonight.  It is getting towards one in the morning when we finally finish.  Very late for the two locals, maybe not so much for the new comers.
