- Somewhere tucked up at the end of a peninsula is an island called "Koh Lanta" in Krabi province, along Thailand’s southwestern coast on the Andaman Sea.  

Geographically, the Andaman Sea is bordered by:

Thailand to the east, Myanmar to the north, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands (India) to the west, and the Strait of Malacca to the south. The Strait of Malacca is one of the most important shipping routes on Earth — the narrow sea lane connecting the Indian Ocean to the South China Sea and the Pacific.

To get from the Andaman Sea into the broader Indian Ocean, you simply sail west or southwest past the Andaman and Nicobar island chain. There isn’t a dramatic “boundary” where one suddenly becomes the other — the Andaman Sea is basically a northeastern arm of the Indian Ocean.

We do find a nice place to get food, and they order some Teh Halia:

Teh Halia is the kind of drink that feels medicinal in the best possible way. Sweet, spicy, creamy, and hot enough to fog your glasses the second it lands on the table.

The name is simple:

Teh = tea
Halia = ginger in Malay

So it’s literally “ginger tea,” but in practice it usually means strong black tea pulled together with fresh ginger, condensed milk or evaporated milk, and enough sugar to make it comforting rather than sharp.
