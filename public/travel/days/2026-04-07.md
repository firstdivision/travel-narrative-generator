## Sunday, April 7

- So, it turns out I didn't need a cab that early
- Security as the Hanedo airport was empty, and I breezed right through. Well, not exactly breezed, for some reason I didnt' think I needed to remove my watch, wallet, or belt.  So I had to do the "Sorry, I'm an idiot" and let them re-scan all that stuff while I walk back through security.
- Spend some time at the Duty Free fragrence and skin-care shop, texting Kate as a guide.  After consultations, it turns out that it is still too expensive and not worth it.
- The boarding commenced on time, they announce there will be no wifi.  Compared to the flight on American Airlines this one is as smooth as butter.
- The food is good.  Noodles with some sort of sauce, and a chicken curry with rice.  See AA? I remember this meal, because it was noteworty.  Maybe *you* should take some *notes*, American Airlines!
- Since there is no wifi I plug in the provided wired earbuds into the seat back and peruse the options. I find two Korea-based movies, "Past Lives", and "People and Meat" [insert a description of these two movies here, and how it's a good way to continue my journey into Asia culture]
- Upon landing I have to run to the next flight which requires a shuttle to a different terminal, and a re-scan through secutity right at the gate, the gate area itself is self-contained behind glass walls.  I wasn't exoecting this, and I was sweaty from the run, but all was well, and soon I was sitting in my seat for the very short flight ti KL.
- The Kuala Lumpur airport is nice.  It's clean, there are plenty of signs, although I follow one sign to "e-rides" only to find that it is not the right one.  The Grab app however has built-in navigation for the airport and it is able to guide me down two esclators, and to the correct Grab pickup spot.
- Again, like walking through Tokyo in the rain, I "do what others do" on the assumption that they know what *they* are doing.  Find the people who look confident, and copy them.  Or like the other expression: Fake it till you make it
- There is a board of monitors with two sections, A and B, Green and Red.  After watching it for a while I realize that the number on the board are not some sort of bizzare random-letter bingo game.  They are the license plates of the e-ride cars that have entered the pickup area.  New ones on the right, and they flow to the left until they disappear, presumably with passengers inside.
- I watch the board, and the car icon on the GRab app on my phone, until the moment arrives.  The car is here.  I exit into a sea of people, immediately disoriented, searching for the correct pickup area, and my car, which should be red.  Then I see it, the plate number matches, I wave, he sees me wave and returns a wave.  But not a "hi, how are you?" wave.  His is saying "Get in here now!", then he vocallizes it, "Hurry!  Get in!".  My first experience in Grab "no wait" zones.  They are not kidding.  Be ready to get in.
- New country time.  I've got that wide-eyed, looking out the window at everything feeling.  There's traffic, and the driver is expertly weaving himself in and out of lanes to get me to my destination quicker. Or maybe for his own amusement. In any case I'm soon deposited at what will become my home for the next 5 weeks.  Tower #2 of "Eclipse @ Pan'gaea".  And immediately I am thrust into my next experience.
- There are guards at this building.  Not just at the main gate. But in this car dropoff area "main lobby" too.  I would learn quickly that this is completely normal in Malaysia, these same guards, dressed in the same uniforms with berets on their heads are in grocery stores, malls.  Seemingly everywhere.  But when you're not expecting it it is a little bit of a shock.
- My instrutions from the AirBNB host said to show a QR code to the guard.  I do this and seems to have no idea why I'm showing him a QR code.  He does show me a log book and asks me to fill out the details.  Name, phone number, passport number --- wait what?  I take pause at the passport number, but then fill it out anyway.  Date, and my signature.  When I'm done I pass the pen back to him, he smiles and says something like "now you are new man", that's what I heard anyway.  Over the next couple days some sort of special bond would form between us, at least in my mind.  On one day he will come over and chat with me about my work, what I do.  "Computers" I will say, and he will ask in return "Ahh, hardware?".  "No, software actually." I'll reply.  He will nod, satisfied with my answer.
- He let me into the elevator lobby, which is behind key-carded locked doors, and he points to the mailroom.  Ahh yes, I nearly forgot.  My keys are in the mailbox, which I unlock with the provided code.
- Elevator up to 19, two right hand turns, and I'm in from of the double doors that make the entranceway to my new abode. The outer door is like a gate, and two feet behind that, with a little landing in between, is the main door.  I figure out which keys open which door, and enter.
- You never know what to expect with things like AirBnB.  They are nearly always "not decorated much", sort of the "bare essentials" (or the Bare Neccecities if you will from a certain animated movie). This one is a little more spartan than I was expecting.  Dual washer/dryer is net to a medium-sized fridge.  White basic countertops, a two burner glasstop electric stove (with the requisite scorch marks and scratches that seem to adorn any glass cooktop over a certain age), a microwave.  To my "American" pre-conceived notion this at first looked barron. A small utilitarian table surrounded by four mis-matched chairs, and a similarly simple living room, two bedrooms, and two bathrooms. So much so that I would send a few texts back to my future travel companions "warning" of the state of it. But over the coming days and weeks I would come to like the simplicity of it.  I would purchase some things that I thought it needed, like the all-important comfy couch blanket so that I can burrito up after coming back from work, and some hand towels for the bathrooms. I would put a little elbow grease into some of the shower grout as well.  But all in all, I dig it, or at least I will eventually.
- I text the host that I am in, and ask about where I might get some basic supplies for the night like water and orange juice, and to let him know that there is a bag of garbage left behind by the cleaning crew (I presume).  He responds immediatley that he'll come pick it up, and get me some supplies.  Soon he arrives with a case of bottles water and two things of orange juice.  I thank him, he responds with a quick Malaysian-style "bow with hands together", I then I am on my own again.
- I soon fall asleep on the couch using one of the blankets from the bedroom.
