## Wednesday, May 13th

- We awake in Bangkok, early, for a ride to BKK airport where we will take a flight to Siam Reap, Cambodia.hurt.
- I eat a DayQuil pill, I am not sure if I am getting sick, but I figure it can't 
- Check in at Bangkok airays counter
- Chill out in Bangkok Airways Lounge
    - Coffee
    - Popcorn, sweet and salty kind, made fresh in one of those carinval style poppers.
    - And discover that our flight to Vietnam from Cambodia that we are supposed to be on the in two days has been rescheduled to "tomorrow", i.e. the same day we land in Cambodia.  This obviously will not work, so we book new tickets on VietAir to take us to Hanoi, Vietnam from Siam Reap.
- Realize that we have spent more time booking thenew flight than we thought, we need to get to our gate!
- Get to gate, take more cold meds, then when we board we get on a bus outside the terminal which drives us quite a long ways to where out plane is parked, in some remote aircraft parking area I guess.
- On the plane we do the NYT crosswords puzzles, which has become somewhat of a tradition on this trip -- to see if we can solve all three (the mini, midi, and big one).
- We are also served an unexpected Japanese curry and rice meal.  When we inquire if it is free the stewardess says "Of course! You should fly Bangkok Airways more often!"
- Land in Cambodia, hit the bathrooms, go to immigration only to discover that our evisas are not all we need.  We need to get real ones, which we can buy at the other side of the room from the immigration lanes.
- Buying the visas is a strange experience, we are shuffled over to a "lane 1" where we give them their passports, and are told abruptly to "Go to lane two!" where we are told to give them our credit cards and "Go to lane 3", in lane three we get everything back, along with slips of recipt paper that are our "evisas".
- Back to the real immigrtion lane where I am told I also need my boarding pass, which I cannot find.  I don't know where it is, maybe on the plane, maybe crumpled up in some forgotten pocket.  I tell the immigration officer I don't have it, he motions for me to just give him my other stuff, which I do.  I am handed back my passport and "visa" and am on my way, now officially "in Cambodia".
- Airport car to Viroth's Villa.  Along the drive I look out the window and see some familiar signs of somewhat impoverished communities go sliding by:
    - Stray dogs with giant nipples swaying under them from having litter after litter of puppies
    - Emaciated livestock trying to eat grass that doesn't exist any more
    - Reminders that even in the US, the majority of the people do not have to endure that level of struggle.
