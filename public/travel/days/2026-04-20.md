## Monday, April 20 

What is Monday at the beginning the work week on this day I joined one of my coworkers to go to the lunch spot next-door, which has a line of mostly permanent. I think food truck/food stalls set up. We both ordered the same thing which is some sort of bone in chicken with rice the name of which I forget and probably did not pronounce correctly when I ordered it . It comes with plastic spoons and knives. That is one thing you have to become accustomed to is being able to get the meat off of something like a bone and chicken thigh with bendy small fragile utensils. I mean, in reality you're supposed to use your hands, but that is one thing I haven't quite been able to get myself to do.  Although as it is, I still ended up using the handwash station, so maybe I should have just gone all in.

Back in Tamarind square I had dinner.  Today was a day to try the Korean place, I decided.

I order Yukkejang because beef sounds good, and the picture on the menu looks like stew, and there's not a giant pile of rice next to it.

It shows up in a heavy black stone bowl, still boiling—really boiling, not just steaming. The surface is actively bubbling, and it stays that way for a while, so you have to be a little careful at first.

The broth is deep and dark from chili oil and flakes and whatever else they put in there, and it smells strongly of beef, garlic, and spice. It’s not subtle. When you look in, it’s a mix of beef, mushrooms, bean sprouts, and greens. There are pieces of something that looked like chicken at first glance, but upon closer inspection was clearly not.  It was the color of chicken but had no fibers. I'm not sure what it was.  Maybe I didn't want to know.

The texture was a big part of it. You get soft, shredded meat, slightly chewy vegetables, and slippery mushrooms all in the same bite. The broth was rich and spicy, but it’s not overwhelmingly heavy.

It came with the standard sides—kimchi, which was sharp and sour and cuts through the richness, and a simple cabbage salad with a light, slightly sweet dressing. Those sides balance the richness of the beef between bites. There was still rice, you cannot completely avoid that, but it was, thankfully, relegated to side-dish and not the feature by quantity.
